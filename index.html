02   Operation
Step-by-step guide
STEP 01
Power on the device

Connect the Raspberry Pi to power. The boot screen shows each component initializing — LED, camera, touch, and model — with a progress bar. The display will briefly show "Ready." before switching to the live camera preview. Wait for the preview to appear before scanning.

STEP 02
Frame the chicken sample

Place the chicken under the camera. The live preview fills the center of the screen. Aim to keep the meat inside the green viewfinder box. The shutter button (circle) sits at the bottom of the screen.

The gear icon (top-right) opens Settings. The grid icon (top-left) opens the Gallery. If the distance sensor is enabled, the status text will appear in the header.

Green viewfinder = sample is in range and ready
Home screen
STEP 03
Tap shutter 3 times to capture

After tapping the shutter, the device captures a photo and shows the capture progress screen. A counter shows how many photos have been taken. Tap the shutter again to take the next photo.

Three filled amber dots indicate all photos are captured. The device moves automatically to the analysis step after the third photo.

Hold steady between taps — 2 s debounce between captures
Capture progress
STEP 04
Wait while the model analyzes

After the third photo, the device automatically enters the analysis phase. The "Analyzing…" screen appears while the AI model runs inference on all three frames and averages the predictions. This typically takes a few seconds. Do not move the device during this step.

Processing
STEP 05
Read the classification result

The result screen shows the captured image above a large colored result label — green for edible, amber for adulterated, red for spoiled. The LED strip on the device glows in the same color.

Use the left/right arrows to navigate through all three captured frames. The session is automatically saved to the Gallery. Tap the shutter button to return to the home screen for a new scan.

03   Classification
Understanding the results
EDIBLE
The chicken appears fresh and suitable for consumption based on visual analysis. Requires ≥ 55% model confidence.
LED → bright green
ADULTERATED
Visual signs suggest the meat may have been mixed with non-chicken material or tampered with. Do not consume.
LED → amber
SPOILED
The chicken shows visible signs of spoilage. Dispose of properly. Do not consume under any circumstances.

04   Navigation
Settings & Gallery
Settings screen

Tap the gear icon (⚙) from the home screen to open Settings. Toggle the distance sensor on or off. Use RELOAD to restart a frozen camera or LED strip without rebooting. Use SHUT DOWN → CONFIRM? to safely power off the device. Tap the shutter to return.

Gallery screen

Tap the grid icon (⊞) from the home screen to open the Gallery. Saved scans are shown as thumbnails grouped by session, newest first. A colored dot at the bottom-left of each thumbnail indicates the classification result. Tap a thumbnail to view the full-size captured frames. Use PREV / NEXT to paginate.

05   Tips
Best practices
→Ensure the LED strip illuminates the sample evenly. Uneven or dim lighting is the most common cause of inaccurate results.
→Place the meat on a neutral, non-reflective surface. Glossy trays or strong background colors can confuse the model.
→Keep the sample inside the green viewfinder box in all three captures for best coverage of the sample.
→If the camera freezes, go to Settings → Camera → RELOAD. This restarts the image pipeline without a full reboot.
→If the LED strip is unresponsive after a scan, go to Settings → LED strip → RELOAD to reinitialize the NeoPixel.
→Always use Settings → Power off → CONFIRM? to shut down. Never cut power abruptly while the camera or model is running.
→Periodically delete old sessions in the Gallery to free up storage space on the device.
